Exploratory Data Analysis

Option Signaling and Resale Value Patterns
in the Taiwan Porsche Macan Market

An EDA project extracting listing data to examine how selected options may influence secondary-market listing prices. This exploratory project uses listing data to examine which options appear to generate stronger resale signals in Taiwan’s Porsche Macan market, and which may function more as first-owner perceived value.

Scope & Methodological Boundaries

  • ▪ Listing vs. Transaction: Evaluates listing-price signals rather than final transaction prices. Reflects market-facing perceived value and seller pricing behavior.
  • ▪ Source Limitation: Data derived from a single major Taiwan resale platform (133 listings). Results should be interpreted as directional signals rather than definitive valuation.
  • ▪ Validation: Options were engineered via NLP from unstructured seller text. A manual spot-check was conducted on sampled listings to validate extraction consistency.
  • ▪ Controlled Variables: Year, Mileage, and explicitly controlled for Vehicle Trim (Base, S, GTS, Turbo).

Model Insights

Strong Resale Signal: Panoramic Sunroof & ACC

The model suggests that sunroofs (+101k TWD) and ACC (+42k TWD) act are associated with stronger positive resale signals in this sample. Features like BOSE audio and 14-way seats do not exhibit positive price signaling. Secondary-market buyers place measurable premiums on spatial perception and standard ADAS functionality.

Brand Authenticity: Sport Chrono

While absolute premiums are modest (+6k TWD), Sport Chrono maintains a positive signal, likely acting as a psychological baseline for brand enthusiasts.

High-Perception / Low-Resale: Comfort Tech

Features like BOSE audio and 14-way seats do not exhibit positive price signaling. These appear to function strictly as first-owner perceived value rather than retrievable investments.

Exploratory Business Implications

This project may offer several discussion points for option positioning and value communication in the Taiwan market.

  • Value Communication: Sales narratives can selectively highlight the historical resilience of features like the Panoramic Sunroof to justify higher initial configuration costs to hesitant buyers.
  • Option Bundling Strategies: High-perception but low-resale features (e.g., premium audio, multi-way seats) may be ideal candidates for targeted promotional bundles, driving new-car margins without heavily disrupting secondary market positioning.
  • Market Monitoring: This NLP-driven pipeline demonstrates a low-cost method to continuously monitor how emerging tech (e.g., ADAS systems) shifts from "premium options" to "expected standards" in the resale ecosystem.
